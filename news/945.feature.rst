``FileSink`` now falls back to plain ``write()`` calls when ``mmap()``-based I/O isn't available (e.g. under restrictive ``RLIMIT_AS`` limits, on filesystems without shared writable mmap support, or when ``MEMRAY_NO_MMAP=1`` is set), allowing capture to continue in those environments. Similarly, if the periodic background memory-sampling thread can't be started due to resource limits, ``Tracker`` creation no longer fails outright; a warning is logged and tracking continues without periodic RSS snapshots.
